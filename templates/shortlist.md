## Cross-cutting findings (read this before sending any inquiries)

### 1. Symphony Green Line station closing June 6, 2026 — for ~3 years
[MBTA announcement](https://www.mbta.com/news/2026-05-06/construction-major-accessibility-improvements-symphony-station-begins-june-6) confirms Symphony station closes for accessibility renovations through Summer 2029. E-line trains will bypass it for Jayshree's entire potential lease term.

**Implications:**
- **Route 39 bus is unaffected** — stops at Mass Ave/Huntington (Symphony Plaza). This is Jayshree's most reliable zero-transfer option from all 5 buildings.
- Green Line E still works, but riders must exit at **Northeastern University** or **Prudential** (~5-min walk to 216 Mass Ave instead of Symphony's 1-min walk).
- This makes **buildings near Route 39 (Bell Olmsted, The Brynx, 95 Saint, Longwood Apts)** more resilient. Atrio's 4-min walk to Symphony becomes a 5-min walk to Mass Ave Orange Line — still fine but slightly less ideal.

### 2. Two of the top picks are 3 blocks apart on S Huntington
Bell Olmsted Park (161 S Huntington) and The Brynx (201 S Huntington) are sister buildings on the JP/Brookline border corridor. Akhil can tour both back-to-back. Both buildings draw from the same hospital/medical-professional applicant pool and both have Route 39 + Green Line E literally at the door.

### 3. Mission Hill data on safety is real but localized
2024 fatal shooting at Tremont/St. Alphonsus; 2025 Tremont shooting near Wentworth. Universal Hub averages ~25 incidents/month in Mission Hill. **BUT:** the violence clusters on the south end (Parker St / Roxbury Crossing); 95 Saint sits on the north end nearer Longwood, which forum consensus calls the safer side of the same street. Longwood Apartments (1575 Tremont) sits closer to Brigham Circle — busier and noisier with hospital traffic.

### 4. The hard-requirement filter is doing its job
3 of 5 buildings (Atrio, Longwood Apartments, plus 95 Saint on budget) fail at least one PRD hard requirement. The remaining two (Bell Olmsted, The Brynx) form a natural focused tour list.
