03 · Cross-cutting findings

Two things to know before you send anything.

⚐

Symphony Green Line station closes June 6, 2026 — for ~3 years.

The MBTA is doing major accessibility renovations through Summer 2029. E-line trains will bypass Symphony for the entire potential lease. Route 39 bus is unaffected — it's the most resilient zero-transfer commute option for Jayshree from all five buildings. If a leasing agent pitches "easy Symphony T access," they're selling slightly outdated info.

⌖

Bell Olmsted and The Brynx are 3 blocks apart.

Both on S Huntington Ave in the same JP/Brookline corridor — the exact stretch the original PRD targeted. Same commute profile, different building character. They can (and should) be toured back-to-back. Hospital-professional applicant pool, Route 39 + Green Line E literally at the door of both.

06 · Decisions needed from you

Five questions before I send anything.

Is $4,500/mo firm?

If you'd stretch to $5,500 for the right place, 95 Saint stays on the tour list. Otherwise it drops.

Mission Hill — intentional re-think, or did it slip in?

95 Saint and Longwood Apts are both in the originally-excluded Mission Hill. If still excluded, both come off the list immediately.

Is in-unit W/D truly hard?

If softened, Atrio's 4-min walk for Jayshree becomes very compelling. (Atrio also has every utility included, which is a real cost offset.)

Jayshree's contact info?

Need her phone, email, and last name for the email signoff block. Or tell me to send solo from Akhil only.

Send the Atrio + Longwood qualifier emails?

They're drafted as short single-question inquiries. Sending costs nothing and closes both loops cleanly. Or skip and just move on.
